Privacy

Your photos never leave your device. Everything Photography Studio does — opening RAW files, editing, healing, exporting — happens right here in your browser, on your hardware. There is no upload, no server-side processing, no account, and no analytics or tracking of any kind.

What the network is used for

  • Downloading the app itself (and its updates) from this site.
  • Downloading a practice photo from this site the first time you tap it, so you can learn on real infrared frames. After that it works offline.

That's the whole list. The app makes no other network requests — your own photos are never sent anywhere, and nothing about you or your editing is collected, measured, or shared.

Location data in your photos

Some cameras and phones save where a photo was taken (GPS) inside the file itself. The editor shows a 🛰 Location saved tip when an opened file carries it, with one-tap ways to save the file — or a copy — without it; a setting can also remove it from the app's working copy automatically whenever a photo opens. Images exported from the editor never include location. Like everything else here, none of this data is read off your device — the check and the removal both happen locally.

What's stored on your device

So the app can work offline and pick up where you left off, it keeps some data locally, in your browser's own storage:

  • Photo sessions you're working on, and finished images from a batch run (crash safety) — removed when you tap Done on a session or save a finished batch.
  • The app itself and any practice photos you've opened, cached for offline use.
  • Your preferences — theme, saved looks, small interface choices.

All of it stays on your device, invisible to anyone else. Clearing this site's data in your browser settings removes every trace.
